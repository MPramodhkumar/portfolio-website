Machine Learning with Python Intern

Teach Nook

June 2023 - August 2023

Worked on an Image Classification project using Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and compared its performance with Artificial Neural Networks (ANNs).

During my internship as a Machine Learning with Python Intern at Teach Nook, the first month involved training sessions I focused on machine learning and Python programming while in the second month, I worked on a project that involved teaching computers to recognize images using a special type of program called a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN).

I used a popular dataset called CIFAR-10, which consists of 50,000 32x32 color training images and 10,000 test images, divided into 10 classes. My goal was to train the computer to recognize what's in each picture, like whether it's a bird, a plane, a vehicle, or something else.

First, I prepared the pictures so that the computer could easily understand them. Then, I tried teaching the computer using a simple method ANN. However, the ANN did not perform very well. The accuracy of the ANN was around 49% only, which means it correctly classified around 49% of the images in the test dataset.

To improve the performance of the model, I could have tried several strategies. For example I used a smarter method called a CNN, which is great for understanding images. This time, the computer did much better at figuring out what was in the pictures.

The performance of the CNN was evaluated on the test data, and the results showed an accuracy of around 80%. This was a significant improvement over the ANN. I compared the two methods and found that the CNN was much better at recognizing things in the pictures. It was like the CNN was a super-smart method compared to the other method.

Overall, the project helped me understand how computers can learn to 'see' and recognize things just like we do.

The project began with collecting historical weather data from a reliable source. After gathering the data, I used Python's Pandas library to load it into a DataFrame.

Then, I used scikit-learn to build and evaluate a linear regression model for predicting rainfall. Finally, I identified key factors influencing rainfall, which could aid in decision-making for agriculture and water management.
